# Creative Brief: Personal Website
**Version 1.0 — April 2026**
*Working document — not for distribution*

---

## Purpose

This site is counter-narrative infrastructure. Its job is to establish an independent read of who this person is before any warm introduction, secondhand account, or categorical assumption does it first.

It is not a résumé. It is not a portfolio in the traditional sense. It is an argument — made through design, restraint, and demonstrated thinking — for a specific kind of value that cannot be compressed into bullet points.

**The conversion does not happen here.** This site generates curiosity and establishes credibility. The next conversation closes the deal.

---

## The One Thing It Must Do

A reader who spends 90 seconds on this site should leave thinking:

> *"I want to talk to this person because of how they think."*

Not: *"I want to hire this person because of what they've shipped."*

---

## Primary Audience

**Hiring managers at creative software and platform companies** — music tech adjacent (Beatport Group, Loopcloud) and broader (Figma-tier creative tools, creator economy platforms). They are evaluating strategic fit, not validating a job description. They are pattern-matchers. The site must create a pattern worth matching.

**Secondary audiences** (site must not break for these):
- Potential consulting clients evaluating POV and trust
- Collaborators/co-founders evaluating whether this person sees things others don't

None of these readers should have to work to understand why this person is relevant to them.

---

## Positioning

**The through-line:**
Someone who has always lived at the intersection of creative practice and the tools that enable it. Not a designer who studied creatives. Not a PM who built for musicians. A practitioner-turned-builder whose practitioner ear never went away.

**The arc reads:**
Years as a successful DJ /Respected producer professional level → Hardware PM (from using tools to shaping them) → Platform/systems thinking → Award-Winning highest-craft instrument design (proof of depth, not ceiling)

**The reframe for the specialist period:**
The UA work was not "synthesizer plugin design." It was designing the cognitive and tactile relationship between a creative person and a tool, at a standard professional musicians defend emotionally. That question — how does a creative person and a tool form a working relationship? — is transferable to any domain where it matters.

**What this is not:**
- A music tech specialist looking for another music tech role
- A niche operator available for specific technical problems
- A backwards-looking evidence document

---

## Relationship to Traditional Documents

The site is not a rebellion against the résumé or LinkedIn profile. It does not signal that those documents are inadequate or that the conventional hiring frame is being rejected. That read would be alienating to exactly the people the site needs to reach.

Instead, the site is an invitation. The traditional documents are the door. This site is the room behind it. A reader who arrives here from a résumé or LinkedIn profile should feel that they have been guided somewhere more interesting — not that they have been handed a manifesto.

The tone is expansive, not defensive. The site trusts that the right reader, given a larger and more interesting story, will reframe what they thought they already knew.

---

## What the Site Should Downplay

**Music-native language and visual signaling** that triggers unconscious categorization before a reader processes a single word. The MI sector is contracting. A reader from outside that world must find this site legible and compelling without any domain context.

**Trophy metrics and outcome claims.** The site does not argue from results. It argues from thinking. "I shipped X and achieved Y" is the résumé. This site shows how problems are held, where attention goes that others' doesn't, and what happens when that cognitive profile is in the room.

**Assertion without demonstration.** "I think in systems" is a claim. A well-constructed process window where systems thinking is visible is proof. Every abstract claim must be anchored by something specific.

**The LinkedIn printout trap.** A site that claims "hire me for how I think" but reads as a formatted CV is self-defeating. The site itself must be an expression of the quality it is claiming.

## Narrative Architecture

### First impression (0–10 seconds)
Disarming. The visual and tonal register should generate: *trustworthy, taste, range, strength* — before a word is processed. Restraint over density. Confidence over credentials.

### The hook (10–30 seconds)
A single framing statement that is not a job title, not a tagline, and not a list of skills. It names the through-line in plain language. It creates a mild cognitive dissonance — *that's not quite what I expected* — that pulls the reader forward.

### The demonstration layer (30–90 seconds)
Two or three examples of thinking made visible. These are not case studies with outcomes. They are process windows: here is how I held a problem, where I looked, what became clear. The UA work lives here — reframed as evidence of a transferable question, not a music industry credential. The insight/critique work (Loopcloud's missing prompt-based search, DJ platform structural vulnerabilities, etc.) lives here as ambient proof of compound perspective — available before any outbound contact is made.
- UA Opal will definitely be one project to showcase, and it has an excellent video trailer: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Jx7lQ7N9jYk

### The résumé anchor (available, not foregrounded)
Credentials, timeline, awards exist on the site — but they are not the argument. They are available for readers who need institutional confirmation of what the thinking layer already established. TEC Awards function here as third-party validation anchors, not as lead claims.

## Voice and Tone

**Direct without being cold.** The intensity that reads as difficult in an ambiguous context reads as rigor when it is applied to something the reader cares about.

**Precise without being dense.** Every sentence earns its place. Nothing is there to fill space or perform depth.

**Confident without trophy-hunting.** The site does not explain itself or seek approval. It presents a perspective and trusts the reader to recognize its value.

**No hedging language.** No "passionate about," no "leverages," no "results-driven." Those phrases signal that the person is performing a role rather than inhabiting one.

**Age-neutral language throughout.** No "over my career," no "decades of experience," no phrasing that prompts the reader to calculate a number. The arc is told as depth accumulation, not chronological distance. Forward orientation — active credentials, current thinking, present-tense framing — is the default register.

---

## Visual Direction

The mood board work in Figma should resolve toward constraints, not just references. Suggested parameters to test:

- **Typography as primary design element (PoR is Plus Jakarta Sans family, focusing on extra light).** The thinking is the content. Type should carry it with authority.
- **Restraint in color.** A palette that reads as considered, not branded. Neither corporate nor music-scene.
- **Negative space as signal.** What is absent tells the reader something. Density would contradict the claim.
- **No portfolio-grid aesthetic.** That visual convention signals "designer looking for work." This site is making a different argument.
- **Performer precedent.** This person has a legitimate performance history. Intentionality of presentation is expected and legible in that context — it does not read as overcalculated.

Proposed sections:
1. **Hook / framing statement** — the through-line, named plainly
2. **How I think** — two or three demonstration pieces; process windows, not case studies
3. **The arc** — compound perspective accumulating: practitioner to tool-shaper to systems thinker. No dates. No math invited. Active credentials appear here as forward-orientation signals.
4. **Selected work** — credentials, awards, products; confirmation layer, not lead argument
5. **Contact / availability signal** — clear, unfussy, not desperate

## Success Criteria

The site is working if:
- A reader with zero music context finds it immediately legible and interesting
- A reader from the target companies forms a prior strong enough to survive a contradictory secondhand account
- The first outbound contact this site enables arrives with the reader already having a frame for the conversation

The site is failing if:
- Any reader's first move is to look for the LinkedIn link to understand who this is
- The music tech context is the lead impression rather than the compound perspective
- The site reads as an explanation of a career rather than an expression of a mind

## Open Questions for the Design Session

- Which two or three pieces of thinking are the demonstration layer? What is their format?
- Where does the DJ/producer history live — foregrounded as the origin story, or present as texture?
- What is the availability signal, and how explicit should it be about the current search?
- Does the site have a name/domain separate from a personal name — and does that decision matter strategically?
